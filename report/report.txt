Parasite is a South Korean black comedy thriller film directed by Bong Joon-ho. It tells the story of the Kims, a poor family who con their way into becoming the servants of the wealthy Park family. As the Kims become more deeply entrenched in the lives of the Parks, they begin to discover dark secrets about the family and their home.

The movie is a commentary on the class divide in South Korea, and the lengths that people will go to in order to improve their social status. The Kims are a resourceful and cunning family, who use their wits to climb the social ladder. However, their success comes at a cost, and they soon find themselves in over their heads.

The movie is also a thriller, with several tense and suspenseful scenes. The tension builds throughout the movie, as the Kims become more and more entangled in the lives of the Parks. The climax of the movie is both shocking and satisfying, and leaves the viewer with a lot to think about.

Overall, Parasite is a well-crafted and thought-provoking movie that is definitely worth watching. It is both entertaining and informative, and will leave you thinking about it long after the credits have rolled.
